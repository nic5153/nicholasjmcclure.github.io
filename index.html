Nicholas J. McClure

Astrophysics Research Assistant | Texas Tech University

I am an astrophysics research assistant at Texas Tech University with a B.S. in physics (astrophysics)/minor in mathematics with a passion for time-domain astronomy, cataclysmic variables, and stellar pulsations. I work primarily with large data sets such as TESS, ZTF, and ground-based observations to investigate periodic variability in compact binary systems and pulsating stars.

My core interests lie in stellar structure and evolution, accretion physics, and asteroseismology with an emphasis in signal processing. I am also passionate about public and academic/scientific outreach, as well as science education. My research experience has given me a strong foundation in astronomical image calibration, observational strategies and planning, public presentation/communication skills, and extensive Python utilization with packages such as NumPy, SciPy, Astropy, Tkinter, Matplotlib and more. My goal is to continue this work by earning a PhD in Astronomy/Astrophysics.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy astrophotography, orchestral and wind ensemble music, and spending time with my dog, Orion, and my cat, Cassie (short for Cassiopeia).

Please feel free to explore this site, view my CV, and contact me at nimcclur@ttu.edu with any questions. Thanks for visiting!

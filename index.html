Welcome

I am a senior at Texas Tech University, set to graduate in December 2025 with a B.S. in Physics and a concentration in Astrophysics. Currently, I work as an undergraduate researcher under Dr. Michael Fausnaugh, where my work focuses on time-domain astronomy, cataclysmic variables, and large dataset analysis.

My core interests lie in stellar evolution and asteroseismology with an emphasis in signal processing. I am also pationate about public and academic/scientific outreach. My research experience has given me a strong foundation in Fourier analysis, astronomical image calibration, observational strategies and planning, public presenation/communication skills and extensive python utilization. My goal is to continue this work by earning a PhD in Astronomy/Astrophysics.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy hiking, camping, and spending quality time with my dog, Orion, and my cat, Cassie (short for Cassiopeia).
